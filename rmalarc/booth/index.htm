Tradeshow Multimedia

LCD Wings and Demo Station for Ellman International

Ellman's booth before showing cluttered workspace, side wings limited to displaying text, hard to change.

Synopsis

Ellman International, a medical equipment company, attends to over 30 medical tradeshows a year. In 2006 they purchased a booth which incorporated the latest style guide. In addition, they wanted to incorporate richer display technologies to enhance the overall appearance of the booth.

In addition, a station to demonstrate their equipment and media materials was needed. Until then, all demos were conducted on a counter or a side table, using a laptop to display the content. I was not aesthetically pleasing and the work space was very crowded.

The project required a modular, easy to setup and transport solution.

 

Solution
Ellman's booth after showing LCD wings displaying graphics and demo stations with LCD screen and light

I decided to replace the brushed aluminum wings with an LCD column with graphics generated by a multimedia computer. This platform allows for rapid customization, enhanced user experience and abilty to display text, graphics and video. Each column was made up of 4 standard 16" LCD screens connected to a computer equipped with a quad VGA video card. Content was developed using Macromedia Flash incorporating text, pictures and video depending on the show. In addition I worked with the engineers and production to design and manufacture a suitable mount for the screens while keeping wire clutter to a minimum. In total, 2 of these wings were used at the booth, one on each side.

For the demo station, I decided to use an existing cart manufactured by ellman and retrofitted it with a custom made arm. I worked on the design of the arm, which features a mount for LCD, halogen lamp for lighting and channels to conceal wiring. I used 21" LCDs with built-in DVD to display clinical footage.
