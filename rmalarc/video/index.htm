Synopsis

As a medical equipment manufacturer, Ellman International, Inc relies heavily on clinical videos to demonstrate and promote their products. in 1999 there was a large collection of over 50 VHS and Betamax videotapes. All videotaping and editing was outsourced. As a result production times on critical marketing videos for new products was very long. In addition, the advent of DVD and digital media threatened to make these assets obsolete.

Solution

Initially, I started converting critical tapes to Video CD format. This format oferred compatibility with both upcoming DVD players and multimedia computers equipped with CD-ROM drives, in addition of low replication costs. As equipment became affordable, DV cameras, video editing and DVD replication equipment was purchased. Over the years a variety of videos and mutimedia materials were produced for a wide array of media, including web, flash and DVD.
